Frequently asked questions

Short answers to what online sellers usually ask before signing up.

Does FluxyOS connect directly to Tokopedia, Shopee, and TikTok Shop?

Yes. FluxyOS pulls orders, payouts, fees, and refunds from Tokopedia, Shopee, TikTok Shop, Lazada, and Blibli. Each marketplace settlement file is parsed automatically so revenue, marketplace fees, and net payouts land on the right ledger lines without manual import.

Can I see profit by SKU or by channel?

Yes. Once COGS, ad spend, and shipping costs enter FluxyOS, every order is automatically split into revenue, marketplace fees, gateway fees, ad cost share, and COGS, so you see true net margin by channel and SKU, not just gross GMV.

How does FluxyOS handle VAT for marketplace orders?

FluxyOS tags each transaction with its VAT treatment based on marketplace settlement formats and your tax setup. The result is an export-ready ledger your accountant or firm can use for monthly VAT reporting without rebuilding a spreadsheet.

What if my store also sells through Shopify or WooCommerce?

FluxyOS treats your D2C website as another channel. Shopify and WooCommerce orders, gateway payouts from Midtrans, Xendit, or Stripe, and refund flows all join the same ledger as marketplace data, so P&L stays consolidated across every storefront you run.

Do I need an accountant to set this up?

No. The default chart of accounts, marketplace fee mappings, and channel categories are preconfigured for Indonesian e-commerce. Connect your store, link your bank, and FluxyOS books the first month for you. Your accountant can review and adjust later.

Is there a free tier for new sellers?

Yes. FluxyOS has a free tier that covers one storefront and basic reconciliation. Paid plans unlock multi-channel ingestion, AI agents, and advanced margin reporting — see the pricing page for details.
